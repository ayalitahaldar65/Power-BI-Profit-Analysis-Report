# Power-BI-Profit-Analysis-Report

This project analyzes product-level profitability using Power BI. The goal was to identify high-performing and underperforming products based on sales, profit, and discounts.

---

## 📂 Dataset

Includes product sales data with:
- Units Sold  
- Sales  
- COGS  
- Discounts  
- Manufacturing Price  
- Date (Year & Month)  

---

## 📊 Key Visuals

- Scatter Chart: Sales vs Profit (bubble size = Units Sold)  
- Trend Analysis: Monthly & Yearly performance  
- KPI Cards: Total Sales, Total Profit, Profit Margin  
- Area Chart: Total Discounts  
- Column Chart: Total Units Sold, Country-wise, Total Sales, Total Profit  

---

## 💡 Key Insights

- A small set of products contributes to a disproportionately high share of total units sold, indicating a strong dependency on key products for volume generation.  

- Discount trends over the years show fluctuations across products, suggesting that certain products rely heavily on discounting strategies to drive sales.  

- While overall sales have increased year over year, profit growth has not always followed the same trend, highlighting margin pressure possibly due to rising COGS or higher discounts.  

- Quarterly analysis reveals variations in gross sales and profit, indicating potential seasonality and periods of higher cost impact.  

- The scatter analysis shows that some products generate high sales but relatively lower profit, pointing to low-margin or high-discount products.  

- Conversely, a few products exhibit strong profitability despite lower sales volumes, indicating high-margin opportunities that can be further scaled.  

- Segment-wise distribution suggests uneven performance across customer segments, with certain segments contributing more effectively to both sales and profit.  

- Units sold do not always translate into higher profitability, reinforcing the importance of optimizing pricing, discounts, and cost structure rather than focusing solely on volume.
